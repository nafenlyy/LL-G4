Impact & Damage to
Communities

Typhoon Ompong caused infrastructure damage worth PHP 52 million and agricultural damage worth PHP 1.65 billion in the affected province, impacting over 40,000 hectares of rice fields and 664 hectares of vegetable plantations. The storm also inflicted significant destruction in Ilocos, Cagayan Valley, Central Luzon, and the Cordillera Administrative Region, which are major agricultural producers in the country with a combined population of over 21 million.

Most typhoons don’t have positive impacts, especially Typhoon Ompong. It was a devastating natural disaster that caused a lot of damage and loss of lives.

Secondary Disasters

Typhoon Ompong brought flash floods, storm surges and landslides. Some 100 small-scale miners are feared dead after a landslide buried their bunkhouse in Itogon, Benguet, during the onslaught of Typhoon “Ompong” in northern Luzon over the weekend. Scores of others have been confirmed killed in floods and landslides, among them two first responders trying to retrieve people trapped in the mud, also in Itogon. At least 42 landslides have been recorded in the Cordilleras, authorities said.

Preparedness & Recovery

The Philippine Red Cross and the government coordinated a massive effort to prepare people for Typhoon Ompong. Warnings were issued to millions of people at risk, who stocked up on supplies and secured their homes. About 350,000 people moved to evacuation centers.

The government, along with National Government Agencies, LGUs, and international organizations, provided relief needs and assistance to affected communities to rebuild their homes and livelihoods.
