Typhoon Ompong

Typhoon Ompong, also known as Typhoon Mangkhut, is the 15th tropical cyclone to affect the country and was named after the mangosteen fruit by the Regional Specialized Meteorological Center in Tokyo. It had sustained wind speeds of more than 205 km/h and gusts of 255 km/h. The typhoon's massive 900 km (560 mi) diameter combined with the seasonal monsoon rains caused intense rainstorms that triggered landslides and flash floods.

Vulnerability of The Philippines

Typhoons and hurricanes, which are often referred to as tropical cyclones, are some of the most severe meteorological phenomena and result in significant expenses and losses worldwide. The combination of warm ocean water, atmospheric conditions, the country's location in the Northwestern Pacific, and unique geography make the Philippines highly vulnerable to typhoons. The Philippines was one of the top 10 nations with the most exposure to disasters, according to research.

Typhoon Ompong Track

Typhoon Ompong followed a westward track towards the Philippines, with its eye making landfall in Baggao, Cagayan, on September 15, 2018. The storm then moved across northern Luzon before exiting the Philippine Area of Responsibility on September 16. Ompong continued to move westward towards southern China and made landfall in Guangdong province on September 16, causing significant damage in the region.
